# Interview Questions

> **Zone:** 2_engine (machine-led prep)
> **When to fill:** after target profile is set.
> **Purpose:** structure conversations to surface past behavior, not future intent.

---

## Interview design principles

These are reminders for the interviewer (you), not part of the question list itself:

- Ask about the **past** ("tell me about the last time you..."), not the future ("would you use..."). Past behavior predicts future behavior; future opinions predict nothing.
- Don't pitch the solution. Don't validate the problem. Listen.
- When the interviewee says something interesting, ask "what did you do next?" — not "how would you feel if...".
- Silence is data. Don't fill it.
- If you find yourself explaining your hypothesis to the interviewee, stop. You're now leading.

## Self-audit checklist

<!-- Before running interviews, audit the question list above against these criteria.
     If Claude is asked to audit, this is the rubric to apply. -->

- [ ] No question contains the words "would you" pointing at a hypothetical future.
- [ ] No question describes the solution or implies what answer is "right".
- [ ] At least 70% of core questions are past-tense and behavioral.
- [ ] Follow-up probes are designed to drill into vague or socially-desirable answers.
- [ ] If multiple personas, separate frameworks exist for each.
